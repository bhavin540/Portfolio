How the work went

Three problems, in the order I hit them.

Situation
What I did
Result

Four states, four sets of books

Sales, dispatch, and stock arrived as separate Excel and POS exports from every depot. No two files agreed on SKU codes or outlet names, so no two reports agreed either.

Built one reporting base

A SQL and Excel consolidation workflow with validation checks, keyed to a clean state → city → outlet → SKU hierarchy that every downstream report could sit on.

~ 75%

less manual prep each cycle, and reconciliation gaps closed for good.

Weak territories hid until month-end

Leadership reviewed static spreadsheets. A city that started slipping in week one surfaced four weeks later — after the month was already lost.

Shipped Power BI dashboards

DAX measures tracking revenue and SKU velocity by state, city, and outlet, refreshed on a weekly cadence instead of rebuilt by hand every month.

Weekly

reviews replaced month-end post-mortems. Slippage got caught inside the week.

Stock was ordered on instinct

Fast movers ran out mid-month in the big cities while dead stock tied up working capital in the small ones. Nobody had looked at demand by city.

Modeled 12 months of demand

City-level seasonality analysis in Python, then ranked every SKU by velocity to produce a reorder-priority list the purchase team could actually use.

40+ SKUs

flagged for clearance, and stockouts on fast movers cut across the territory.

The through-line to everything on my projects page: get the data into one trustworthy shape, find the thing the business can act on, then put it somewhere people will actually look.
